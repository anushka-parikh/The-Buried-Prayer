The Buried Prayer

I hear the words, but can't reply, A distant echo, a silent cry. I am here, but I am not, A soul lost, a life forgot.
My dream that doesn't end, A shadow I can't break or bend. I touch light that slips away, Lives in the darkness, my lost pray.
Too scattered to be shattered, A soul too worn to feel mattered. Broken into pieces, pieces to dust, I wait to bleed, in void and disgust.
Fading like the echoes of my screams, Deafened by the silence of my dreams. A fallow heart that can't bear a root, Where hope once whispered, now mute.
